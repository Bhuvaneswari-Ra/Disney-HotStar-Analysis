## What the Data Revealed

### 43% of Catalogue Targets U/A 13+ — Kids Content Underserved
> **2,980 of 6,874 titles** are rated U/A 13+, while U-rated
> (family-friendly) content accounts for only **1,251 titles** —
> revealing a significant content gap in the kids and family segment

### Drama Leads as the Single Largest Genre
> Drama holds **2,005 distinct titles** — the largest content
> category on the platform, giving acquisition teams a clear
> benchmark for catalogue depth and competitive positioning

### Average Runtime Sits at 98.75 Minutes
> Combined with runtime distribution analysis, this baseline
> reveals where content length aligns or conflicts with
> engagement patterns across age groups

### 37 Genres Tracked Across 95 Years of Releases (1928–2023)
> Genre-year analysis surfaces which categories have grown,
> plateaued, or declined over nearly a century of content —
> a direct input for long-term portfolio strategy

### Comedy Outnumbers Action by 32%
> Comedy **(787 titles)** vs Action **(596 titles)** — dedicated
> deep-dive pages for both genres surface runtime and age rating
> differences that generic genre charts miss

## Project Overview

Independent analytical project built from a structured business
brief — analyzing Disney+ Hotstar's movie catalogue across genres,
runtimes, release years, age ratings, and content types to surface
insights for content strategy, audience segmentation, and
marketing teams.

| Detail | Info |

| Tasks | 12 Business Tasks Completed |

---

## Problem Statement

Disney+ Hotstar's movie catalogue — spanning 37 genres, multiple
content types, age ratings, and 95 years of releases — existed in
raw, disconnected form. Without a unified analytical view, key
patterns in audience preferences, content gaps, and release trends
remained hidden, slowing decisions across production, acquisition,
and marketing.

---

## Approach

- Profiled 6,874 titles, resolving inconsistencies across genre,
  runtime, age rating, and episode fields
- Created runtime bins and calculated fields to enable
  distribution and segmentation analysis
- Built dedicated deep-dive pages for Action (596 titles) and
  Comedy (787 titles) — the two highest-priority genres — with
  genre-specific runtime, year, and age rating breakdowns
- Designed GENRE, GENRE INSIGHTS, GENREPOP, and DEEP DIVE pages
  covering distribution, popularity trends, and correlation analysis
- Completed all 12 business tasks including top 10 longest movies,
  genre popularity over time, and runtime-year correlation
